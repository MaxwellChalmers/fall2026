# Course Context

## Working course frame

This course examines AI as a technical, social, political, and environmental system. Students use hands-on labs to demystify core AI concepts while also studying how AI systems are shaped by ideology, institutions, infrastructure, labor, environmental costs, and governance choices.

The course asks not only how AI works, but also:

- why AI is often framed as intelligent, alive, inevitable, or neutral
- who benefits from AI deployment
- what forms of dependency AI creates
- what harms are distributed through classification, prediction, monitoring, and automation
- how emerging technologies should be governed before their impacts are fully known

## Weekly rhythm

The course meets twice per week for 100 minutes.

Preferred structure:

- first meeting: critical lens / seminar / reading discussion
- second meeting: lab / applied activity / synthesis
